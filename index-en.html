Projects

  • Cruzcampo Campaign

    • Andalusia’s water

      In the subject of Discursive Strategies at the University of Seville, the final work consists of the creation of a complete campaign for an existing brand, and the subsequent linguistic analysis of the same. However, all the creative effort is focused on giving a twist to this relationship that is automatically formed in our minds: Cruzcampo-Andalusia, Andalusia-party.

      Based on the 2009 campaign "Made in Andalusia" by Bassat Ogilvy, this reinterpretation of Andalusia seeks to move away from the stereotypical party to find the other side of the south. This Andalusia is much more than abanicos and tapas, it is its most natural and pure part, it is its water; the same water that gives that characteristic flavor to this brand's beer. This is where Cruzcampo's commitment to its geography and environment comes into play, to vindicate the purity of its flavor, the “water of Andalusia”, another source of pride for this land.

      The objectives of the campaign were:

      • To change the public's vision of Andalusia: introducing in addition to festivities and culture, concepts that revolve around water and environment.
      • - Raise awareness of the importance of keeping the water clean.

      Water is life, we are 70% water. Cruzcampo beer is a way of enjoying life, it is now the “water of Andalusia”.

  • The Real Challenge

    • The biggest challenge is to stay at home

      At the beginning of the pandemic, back in March 2019, when the professors did not yet know that the initial two weeks of confinement would last for months, in one of the courses at the University of Seville we were asked to create a video. There were not many instructions, only that it had to stick to the COVID-19 theme: it could be a video blog, an activity for children -a long etcetera- or a corporate video of any company or institution. I opted for the last one.

      "The Real Challenge" is a video campaign for the Instagram application. When boredom just started to become latent, thousands of people found entertainment on social networks, participating in viral challenges that kept their minds distracted for a few hours. The video I present here was made in my backyard with the collaboration and effort of my two flatmates -thanks Carmen and Juan. The official version that Instagram would present as an ad in stories or posts would be made up of fragments of challenges from different users of the social network. The name of the campaign, as the claim at the end explains, alludes to all the viral challenges and puts the focus on the biggest challenge of all: staying at home.
